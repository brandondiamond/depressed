Get the facts

Depression is a medical condition

  • It's not a choice: depression changes how we think and feel.1
  • Depression isn't just sadness— it can feel like lasting emptiness.1
  • Neuroanatomy plays a role in depression,1 as do genetics.6
  • Depressed brains are often physically different from non-depressed brains.2

Depression impacts nyc

  • Major depressive disorder is the greatest source of disability in NYC.3
  • 8% of men and 10% of women in NYC live with depression.3
  • Depression contributes to $14 billion in annual losses, citywide.4
  • At least 1 in 5 new yorkers will experience a mental health disorder this year.3

Depression can affect anyone

  • Anyone can experience depression, even "successful" people.
  • Women are twice as likely to experience depression in the US.3
  • Some jobs increase the risk (especially real estate, law, and transit).5
  • Underserved communities are 2-3x more vulnerable and less likely to get help.3

Depression is treatable

  • 80% of people in treatment see improvement in 4-6 weeks.7
  • … But two thirds of new yorkers don't get treatment.3
  • Younger new yorkers are about half as likely to seek help.3
  • Stigma and embarassment are major obstacles to getting care.8

Stigma is a huge problem

  • Stigma has serious negative effects on hope and self-esteem.9
  • Misconceptions lead to isolation and embarassment.
  • Isolation makes depression symptoms much worse.10
  • Stigma is often internalized, making it harder to seek help.11

Learn the symptoms

Depression is more than sadness. If you've been experiencing any of these symptoms for more than two weeks, you should talk with your doctor.

Emotional

  • Feeling numb, empty, or sad.
  • Feeling guilty or worthless.
  • Feeling hopeless or pessimistic.
  • Feeling anxious or angry.

Physical

  • Lasting fatigue or low energy.
  • Problems with sleep.
  • Changes in weight or appetite.
  • Trouble remembering or focusing.

Lifestyle

  • Isolating yourself.
  • Losing interest in hobbies.
  • Avoiding friends and family.
  • Difficulty making decisions.

Urgent

  • Thoughts of death or suicide.
  • Thoughts of self-harm.
  • Physical pain.
  • Get confidential help.

Sex differences1

  • Women are more likely to feel sadness, worthlessness, and guilt.
  • Men are more likely to feel tired, irritable, and to lose interest in activities.

Learn about treatment

There are many options for treating depression. In fact, 80% of people see improvement from treatment in the first few weeks.7

The best way to start feeling better is to meet with your regular doctor. They'll help you put together an effective treatment plan.

Next steps

Therapy
Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
CBT is a highly effective12 type of talk therapy that helps you change your thought process.
Learn more
Therapy
Talk therapy
There are many types of talk therapy that help with depression. Both one-on-one and group-based sessions are effective.

Exam
Physical exam
Depression can be caused by diet13, hormones14, or a number of medical conditions. It's important to rule these out early on.
Learn more

Lifestyle changes

Lifestyle
Eat healthy
Issues with your diet can lead to depression.13 Your doctor can help you identify any deficiencies.
Learn more
Lifestyle
Get exercise
Aerobic exercise like taking a walk or spending time at the gym can reduce depression.15
Learn more
Lifestyle
Mindfulness
Mindfulness is a way of thinking that can help rewire your brain16 and reduce the risk of relapse by half.17

Medications

Medication
Antidepressants
The right antidepressant can make a big difference.18 Your doctor can help you choose the best medicine and dosage for your symptoms.
Learn more
Exam
Genetic testing
New genetic tests can help reduce side effects and identify the medications that'll work best for you.19
Learn more
Medication
Ketamine
Ketamine is an effective20 new option for treatment-resistent depression that works quickly.

More options

Procedure
Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS)
A simple, in-office treatment that provides 50-60%21 of patients with relief.
Learn more
Procedure
Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)
ECT is a highly effective,22 in-patient option for severe, treatment-resistent depression.
Learn more
Procedure
Electrostimulation therapy
Vagus nerve stimulation (VNS) and deep brain stimulation (DBS) can help treat certain types of depression.
Learn more

New research

Medication
Psilocybin
The FDA recently granted psylocibin "breakthrough therapy" status after a successful feasibility study.23
Learn more
Medication
Microdosing
Initial research shows that small (i.e., non-hallucinatory) doses of psychedelics may help relieve depression.24
Learn more
Medication
Anti-inflammatory medicine
Several studies have identified a promising link between depression and inflammation.25
Learn more
Procedure
Fecal microbiota transplant (FMT)
