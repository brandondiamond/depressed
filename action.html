Fight stigma, fight depression.

Stigma destroys hope and self-esteem, making symptoms worse and treatment harder. Let's become visible.1

Five steps to fight depression in NYC:

  1. Spread the Word.
  2. Become Visible.
  3. Meet in Person.
  4. Tell Your Story.
  5. Volunteer.

1. Spread the word. top ↑

Online visibility helps. We use our social media accounts to share facts and stories about depression in NYC. By following and sharing, you can help us reach more people.

Follow us on InstagramFollow us on Facebook

2. Become visible. top ↑

Show your support. If you are or have been depressed— or just want to fight the stigma— wear a black bracelet to show your support.

Anything works: a hair tie, some string, a rubberband. It's about becoming visible.

Get shirts and stickers. We've designed gear that you can wear to show your support. We never profit from sales.

Get our Gear

3. Meet in person. top ↑

Meet us in person. Our events are low commitment and fun. Sometimes we'll have speakers, sometimes we'll just hang out. Every month, we host a visibility night where we all get together in union square.

Upcoming Events

4. Tell your story. top ↑

Share your experience. We're collecting stories from new yorkers living with depression. By telling your story, you can remind other people that they're not alone.

Share your Story

5. Join the team. top ↑

Build the movement. This is an entirely independent (and 100% volunteer) social movement. If you'd like to get involved, we'd love to have you.

Get involved

Feeling overwhelmed? Call or text NYC Well for confidential help.

If you or someone you know is considering suicide, call the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-8255 or, in an emergency, 911.
